## 1. Purpose

This Internal Audit Programme defines the planned schedule, scope, methodology, and resources for conducting PIMS internal audits in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Clause 9.2. It ensures systematic, periodic evaluation of the PIMS to confirm its conformance, effectiveness, and alignment with legal and regulatory requirements.

---

## 2. Audit Objectives

The PIMS internal audit programme aims to:
- Verify conformance of the PIMS with ISO/IEC 27701:2025 requirements
- Assess compliance with GDPR, UAE PDPL, and other applicable regulations
- Evaluate the effectiveness of implemented privacy controls
- Identify non-conformities, gaps, and opportunities for improvement
- Provide assurance to senior management and the DPO
- Support preparation for external certification audits

---

## 3. Audit Scope

The audit scope covers all areas of the PIMS, including:
- PIMS policies, procedures, and documented information (Clause 7.5)
- Context of the organisation (Clause 4)
- Leadership and governance (Clause 5)
- Privacy risk management (Clause 6)
- Support processes — training, communications, document control (Clause 7)
- Operational controls — DPIA, DSR, breach response, consent, retention (Clause 8)
- Annex A controls (PII Controller) and Annex B controls (PII Processor)
- Third-party processor management
- Performance monitoring and measurement (Clause 9.1)

---

## 4. Audit Frequency and Risk-Based Planning

Audit frequency is determined by:
- Criticality of the area (high-risk processes audited more frequently)
- Previous audit findings (areas with NCs audited at next cycle)
- Regulatory requirements or changes
- Changes to systems, processes, or personnel

| Audit Area | Risk Level | Audit Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| DPIA process and outcomes | High | Annual |
| Data subject rights procedure | High | Annual |
| Privacy breach response | High | Annual |
| Consent management | High | Annual |
| Third-party processor management | High | Annual |
| Cross-border transfers | High | Annual |
| Risk register and treatment plan | High | Annual |
| Training and awareness | Medium | Annual |
| Document control | Medium | Annual |
| Retention and disposal | Medium | Annual |
| Privacy by design adoption | Medium | Annual |
| SOA currency and control implementation | High | Annual |
| Leadership and governance | Medium | Biennial |
| Privacy policy review cycle | Medium | Biennial |

## 6. Audit Methodology

### 6.1 Audit Types

| Type | Description |
|------|------------|
| **Documentation Review** | Review of policies, procedures, records, and documented information |
| **Interviews** | Structured interviews with process owners, DPO, staff |
| **Observation** | Observation of processes (e.g., consent capture, DSR handling) |
| **Technical Testing** | Verification of technical controls (e.g., encryption, access controls) |
| **Sampling** | Sample testing of records (e.g., DSR logs, consent records, disposal records) |

### 6.2 Finding Classifications

| Classification | Description | Response Time |
|---------------|------------|--------------|
| **Major NC** | Systematic absence or failure of a required control; could lead to regulatory breach | 30 days |
| **Minor NC** | Isolated failure or gap; low immediate risk | 60 days |
| **Observation** | Opportunity for improvement; not a conformance failure | Next audit cycle |
| **Positive Finding** | Good practice identified for sharing | Document and promote |

## 7. Auditor Competence Requirements

Internal auditors conducting PIMS audits must:
- Be independent of the area being audited
- Hold or be working towards ISO 27701 Lead Auditor qualification (or equivalent)
- Have working knowledge of GDPR and applicable data protection law
- Understand the organisation's PIMS policies and procedures
- Have completed PIMS auditor briefing with the DPO

External auditors may be engaged for specialist areas or if independence cannot be maintained.

## 8. Audit Evidence Requirements

| Evidence Type | Examples |
|-------------|---------|
| Documented information | Policies, procedures, registers, records |
| Interview notes | Signed records of key interviews |
| Sample records | DSR logs, consent records, DPIA records |
| Technical evidence | Screenshots, system reports, access control lists |
| Supplier documents | DPAs, supplier assessment reports, certifications |

---

## 9. Audit Reporting and Follow-Up

1. **Draft Report**: Issued to auditee within 10 business days of audit completion
2. **Auditee Review**: Auditee has 5 business days to comment on factual accuracy
3. **Final Report**: Issued to DPO and Senior Management within 15 business days
4. **Corrective Action Plan**: Due from auditee within agreed response time (see Section 6.2)
5. **Follow-Up Review**: Auditor verifies corrective action evidence within 30 days of deadline
6. **Closure**: Finding closed when evidence of effective correction confirmed
7. **Management Review Input**: All audit findings reported at annual Management Review
