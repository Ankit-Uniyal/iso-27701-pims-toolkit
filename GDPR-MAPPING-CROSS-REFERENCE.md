## Purpose

This document cross-references **ISO/IEC 27701:2025** clauses and Annex A/B controls with the corresponding **GDPR (EU) 2016/679** articles. It is intended to help:

- DPOs and privacy practitioners understand how PIMS implementation satisfies GDPR obligations
- Auditors trace GDPR compliance evidence through the PIMS framework
- Organisations demonstrate GDPR accountability through ISO 27701:2025 certification

> **Note:** ISO 27701:2025 certification does not constitute automatic GDPR compliance. However, it provides a structured accountability mechanism that supports GDPR compliance demonstration under Art. 24 and 42.

## Part 10: GDPR vs. ISO 27701:2025 — Overall Compliance Value

| GDPR Requirement | Is ISO 27701:2025 Certification Sufficient? | Notes |
|-----------------|------------------------------------------|-------|
| Art. 5 principles | Significantly supports | PIMS embeds all six principles |
| Art. 6 lawful basis | Supports — not sufficient alone | PIMS requires lawful basis to be identified; legal assessment required |
| Art. 13–14 notices | Supports | Privacy notice required by A.7.6.1 |
| Art. 15–22 rights | Significantly supports | All rights covered by Annex A |
| Art. 25 PbD | Fully supports | A.7.2.8 + B.8.3.1 are standalone 2025 controls |
| Art. 28 processors | Fully supports | Annex B + DPA Template |
| Art. 30 ROPA | Fully supports | PIMS requires comprehensive ROPA |
| Art. 32 security | Supports (via ISO 27001:2022 base) | ISO 27001:2022 + PIMS = strong Art. 32 demonstration |
| Art. 33–34 breach | Fully supports | Breach procedure covers 72-hour rule |
| Art. 35 DPIA | Fully supports | Enhanced DPIA in 27701:2025 |
| Art. 37 DPO | Supports | DPO ToR in PIMS; appointment is a legal question |
