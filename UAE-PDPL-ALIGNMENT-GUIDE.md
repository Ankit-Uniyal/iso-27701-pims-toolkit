## 1. Overview of UAE PDPL

The **UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data** entered into force in January 2022, with the executive regulations issued in 2023. The PDPL is the UAE's first comprehensive federal personal data protection law.

**Key Regulatory Body:** UAE Data Office (established under the PDPL)  
**Scope:** Applies to processing of personal data of individuals in the UAE by organisations operating in the UAE  
**Territorial Scope:** UAE mainland; note that DIFC (Dubai International Financial Centre) has its own separate data protection law (DIFC DP Law 2020), as does ADGM.

## 5. DIFC Data Protection Law Note

Organisations operating within the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) are subject to the **DIFC Data Protection Law 2020 (DIFC Law No. 5 of 2020)** rather than the federal PDPL. The DIFC law is closely modelled on GDPR and most toolkit documents apply with minor adaptations.

Key differences for DIFC:
- DIFC Commissioner of Data Protection (not UAE Data Office)
- Higher alignment with GDPR — most GDPR guidance applies
- DIFC Data Protection Register requirements

Organisations in Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM) are subject to the **ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021** — also closely GDPR-aligned.

## 6. Practical Guidance for UAE Organisations Using This Toolkit

1. Use all toolkit documents as your foundation — they are substantially compatible with UAE PDPL
2. Add UAE PDPL-specific requirements as a supplement layer (this document + local legal advice)
3. Ensure privacy notices are available in Arabic for UAE-resident data subjects
4. Register your Personal Data Officer with the UAE Data Office when required
5. Check UAE Data Office guidance for any sector-specific regulations (healthcare, financial services)
6. Monitor the UAE Data Office website for implementing regulations and guidance notes
