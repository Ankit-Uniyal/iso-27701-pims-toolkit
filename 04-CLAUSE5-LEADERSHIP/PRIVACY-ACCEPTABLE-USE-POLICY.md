# Privacy Acceptable Use Policy

**Document ID:** PIMS-LDR-004
**Version:** 1.0 | **Classification:** Internal | **Date:** 2025
**Owner:** DPO | **Review Date:** Annual

---

## Purpose

This policy governs acceptable use of PII by all employees, contractors, and third parties who access PII at [Organisation Name].

---

## Acceptable Use Rules

### 2.1 Access to PII
- Access only PII necessary for your job function
- Use only authorised [Organisation Name] systems
- Never share login credentials; use MFA where required

### 2.2 Handling PII
- Process PII only for authorised purposes documented in the ROPA
- Collect only the minimum PII necessary (data minimisation)
- Keep PII accurate — report inaccuracies to the Privacy Team
- Do not create unauthorised copies in personal drives or cloud storage

### 2.3 Sharing PII
- Internal: Share only with colleagues with legitimate need
- External: Never share without DPO authorisation and a DPA in place
- Never share on public platforms or unencrypted channels
- Verify email recipients before sending PII

### 2.4 Transferring PII
- Never use personal email to send PII
- Encrypt all PII in transit using approved tools
- Physical documents: transport securely, never leave unattended

### 2.5 Storing PII
- Use only approved storage locations (internal SharePoint, approved CRM/HR system)
- Never store PII on personal cloud or unencrypted local drives
- Clear desk / clear screen at all times

### 2.6 Retaining and Disposing of PII
- Follow retention schedules per the Data Retention and Disposal Policy
- Paper with PII: shred using cross-cut shredders
- Digital PII: use approved secure deletion tools

### 2.7 Data Subject Rights
- If anyone requests to see, correct, or delete their data, escalate to DPO immediately
- There are legal deadlines (1 month) — delay is not an option

---

## Prohibited Activities

- Accessing PII you are not authorised to access
- Using PII for personal gain
- Sharing PII with unauthorised parties
- Circumventing access controls or privacy safeguards
- Processing PII without a valid lawful basis
- Creating shadow copies outside approved systems
- Selling or commercialising any PII

---

## Reporting Obligations

All staff must **immediately report** to [privacy@organisation.com]:
- Any suspected privacy breach
- Any lost or stolen device containing PII
- Any unusual request for access to PII
- Any unsolicited external request for PII

**The 72-hour regulatory clock starts when a breach is discovered. Immediate reporting is mandatory.**

---

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal, personal liability under data protection law, and in serious cases, criminal prosecution.
