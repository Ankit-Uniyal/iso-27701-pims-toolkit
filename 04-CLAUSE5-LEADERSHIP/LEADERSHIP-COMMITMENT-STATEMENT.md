## Statement of Commitment

[Organisation Name] is committed to the protection of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and to the implementation and continual improvement of a Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) in accordance with ISO/IEC 27701:2025.

Top management of [Organisation Name] hereby commits to:

---

## 1. Privacy Policy and Objectives

- Establishing, approving, and communicating a Privacy Policy that is appropriate to the purpose of the organisation
- - Setting measurable Privacy Objectives aligned to the Privacy Policy and the requirements of ISO/IEC 27701:2025
  - - Ensuring the Privacy Policy and objectives are reviewed at least annually
   
    - ---

    ## 2. Integration with Business Processes

    - Integrating PIMS requirements into the organisation's business processes and strategic planning
    - - Ensuring that privacy considerations are embedded in all new systems, products, and services from the outset (Privacy by Design — ISO 31700)
      - - Recognising that the PIMS extends the organisation's ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Information Security Management System
       
        - ---

        ## 3. Resources and Competence

        - Providing the necessary resources — financial, human, and technological — for effective PIMS implementation, operation, and maintenance
        - - Appointing a qualified Data Protection Officer (DPO) with sufficient independence, authority, and resources
          - - Ensuring all staff who handle PII receive appropriate privacy awareness training and that competence requirements are maintained
           
            - ---

            ## 4. Legal and Regulatory Compliance

            - Ensuring the organisation identifies, documents, and complies with all applicable privacy laws and regulations, including GDPR, UK GDPR, UAE PDPL, India DPDPA 2023, and other applicable regulations
            - - Supporting timely notification to regulatory authorities in the event of a PII breach
              - - Ensuring that data subject rights are respected and fulfilled within regulatory deadlines
               
                - ---

                ## 5. Third-Party and Supply Chain Privacy

                - Requiring that all third-party processors who handle PII on behalf of [Organisation Name] are subject to adequate contractual safeguards and due diligence
                - - Ensuring that joint controller arrangements are formally documented and responsibilities are clearly allocated per ISO/IEC 27701:2025 Control A.7.2.7
                  - - Supporting Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs) for cross-border PII transfers where required
                   
                    - ---

                    ## 6. Continual Improvement

                    - Promoting a culture of privacy awareness and accountability throughout the organisation
                    - - Supporting the conduct of regular PIMS internal audits, management reviews, and risk assessments
                      - - Ensuring that nonconformities are corrected and that the PIMS is continually improved
                       
                        - ---

                        ## 7. Management Review

                        Top management shall participate in at least one formal PIMS Management Review annually, reviewing the suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the PIMS and approving any required changes or improvements.
